Your app is a **multi-tenant workforce platform**: attendance (PIN clock-in), payroll, employee management, branches, and admin tooling. That’s broader than “timesheet,” so the strongest names suggest **workforce / shifts / payroll** without being purely descriptive.

## What makes a name easier to trademark

| Stronger | Weaker (hard to protect) |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Coined or invented words (`Deputy`, `Gusto`) | Generic descriptors (`Time Tracker`, `Work Hours`) |
| Suggestive but not literal (`Rippling`, `Lattice`) | Geographic or personal names tied to old branding |
| Short, distinctive, easy to spell | Common dictionary words alone (`Shift`, `Clock`, `Pay`) |

Before you commit: run a **trademark search** (USPTO, EUIPO, or your jurisdiction) and check **domain + app store** availability. I’m not a lawyer — a quick clearance search with a trademark attorney is worth it before filing.
